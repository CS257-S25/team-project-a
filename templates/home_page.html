Data Overview:

Drug addiction and abuse remain one of the most urgent public health crises in the United States. Every year, millions of Americans struggle with disorders caused by substance abuse. Casual recreational use could ultimately lead to fatal addiction.

The human cost is proliferating: overdose deaths have surged in recent years, reaching over 100,000 annually.

Moreover, drug abuse has also showed flaws in the criminal justice system. One infamous case was the 2015 death of Freddie Gray in Baltimore, who was arrested in a high-drug-use area and died in police custody, triggering national protests over law enforcement practices. Cases like this emphasized how drug-related arrests often intersect with bias and radical forces, raising urgent questions about justice and public safety.

This website is dedicated to presenting statistics from survey on people who suffered from drug abuse and as a result, have been arrested. The dataset contains self-reported statistics from women who are arrested at least one tiem on drug usage or sale charges.

Authored by:
Carl Leukefeld, University of Kentucky. Center on Drug and Alcohol Research

Geographic Coverage:
Rhode Island, Conneticut, Kentucky, Delaware

Exact name of the dataset and version:

Version: V1

User Guide:

Are you curious about how many times a drug dealer sells drugs 30 days prior to their arrest? Use the "Arrest" tab and put in the number of drug deals made in the past 30 days, and check out how many of them are selling that much within the range.

To check the statistics for self-help meetings, click on "Meeting Info" button to check the average and total counts.

The top bar can be used for navigation by clicking on the buttons along the bar underneath the title of the page. Additionally, there is a search bar where it is possible to look for a specific page. To use the search bar put in a page name, and you can use it to navigate to that page.

The "Graph" tab can be used to check the statistics of the self-help meeting attendance and effect of drugs on people's mental health. The graph shows you the correlation between people's mental status and self-help meeting attendence.

The "Drug Info" tab can be used to check the statistics of the drug usage and its effect on people's mental health and physical health. The "Alcohol Info" tab also shows alcohol's effect on people's mental health and physical health. Both are rated in a scale out of 5.
