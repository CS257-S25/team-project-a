Info:

This is a site designed to facilitate the analysis of a survey on people who have suffered from drug abuse and as a result have been arrested. It contains slf reportated statistics from women who were arrested at least one time on drug usage or sale charges.

Authored by:
Carl Leukefeld, University of Kentucky. Center on Drug and Alcohol Research

Geographic Coverage:
Rhode Island, Conneticut, Kentucky, Delaware

Exact name of the dataset and version:
Criminal Justice Drug Abuse Treatment Studies (CJ-DATS): Restructuring Risky Relationships-HIV (RRR-HIV), 2005-2008 [United States] (ICPSR 30842)
Version: V1

User Guide:

Are you curious about how many times a drug dealer sells drugs 30 days prior to their arrest? Use the "Arrest" tab and put in the number of drug deals made in the past 30 days, and check out how many of them are selling that much within the range.

To check the statistics for self-help meetings, click on "Meeting Info" button to check the average and total counts.

The top bar can be used for navigaion by clicking on the buttons along the bar underneath the title of the page. Additonaly there is a search bar where it is possible to look for a specific page. To use the search bar put in a page name and you can use it to navigate to that page.
